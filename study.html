Foundations in Maths
📘 Stroud — Engineering Mathematics (7th ed)
Stroud is split into two parts. Part I (Foundation programmes F.1–F.13) covers everything from basic arithmetic up to intro calculus — start here regardless of how you feel about your level. Part II (Programmes 1+) is first-year degree level. Work through in order — each programme builds on the last. Once you finish F.13, you're ready to open Kreyszig.
Part I — do first (build your foundation)
F.1
Arithmetic
Numbers, fractions, powers, rounding. Start here even if it feels basic — gaps here cause problems everywhere else.
Step 1
F.2
Introduction to Algebra
Variables, expanding brackets, powers, logarithms, algebraic fractions. Critical groundwork.
Step 2
F.3
Expressions & Equations
Rearranging and solving equations. You'll do this constantly in every engineering subject.
Step 3
F.4
Graphs
Plotting equations, Cartesian axes, inequalities. Underpins visualising everything in maths and engineering.
Step 4
F.5–F.6
Linear & Polynomial Equations
Simultaneous equations, quadratics. Used heavily in circuit analysis and statics.
Step 5
F.9
Trigonometry
Sin, cos, tan, identities, triangles. Essential for ENGE1000 (AC circuits) and mechanics.
Step 6
F.11
Trigonometric & Exponential Functions
Graphs and behaviour of trig and e^x functions. Directly feeds into ODEs and AC circuits.
Step 7
F.12
Differentiation
Derivatives, chain rule, product rule. The gateway to everything in ENGI1004.
Step 8
F.13
Integration
Antiderivatives, definite integrals, substitution. Needed for solving ODEs — do this before touching Kreyszig.
Step 9
Part II — do after (once foundation is solid)
Prog 3
Hyperbolic Functions
Sinh, cosh, tanh — appear in ODE solutions. Short programme, do it when you hit them in Kreyszig.
Prog 5
Matrices & Determinants
Directly supports Kreyszig Ch 7. Do this before or alongside that chapter.
Prog 10–11
Further Differentiation & Integration
Partial derivatives, integration by parts, partial fractions. Needed for more advanced ODE solving.
Prog 24–25
Statistics & Probability
Revisit to reinforce what you did in MATH1000 last semester.
Skip for now
F.7–F.8
Binomials & Partial Fractions
Useful eventually but not urgent — come back when needed.
Prog 1–2
Complex Numbers
Important later (AC circuits, ENGI1004) but not your immediate priority.
Prog 12–23
Series, Transforms, Vector Calculus, Numerical Methods…
Second year material — leave for now.
ENGI1004 — Engineering Modelling 1
📘 Kreyszig — Advanced Engineering Mathematics (9th ed)
Complete the Stroud foundation programmes — especially F.12–F.13 on differentiation and integration — before starting here. Kreyszig assumes you can already differentiate and integrate confidently.
Do first — core for ENGI1004
Ch 1
First-Order ODEs
Separable, linear, integrating factors. The foundation — everything else builds on this.
Step 1
Ch 2
Second-Order Linear ODEs
Constant coefficients, homogeneous equations, mass-spring modelling. Big chapter — take your time.
Step 2
Ch 7
Linear Algebra: Matrices & Linear Systems
Matrix operations, determinants, solving systems of equations. Do before Ch 4.
Step 3
Ch 6
Laplace Transforms
Most useful engineering tool for solving ODEs. Very likely in scope for this course.
Step 4
Ch 4
Systems of ODEs
Combines Ch 2 and Ch 7 knowledge. Requires matrices — do Ch 7 first.
Step 5
Skip for now
Ch 3
Higher-Order ODEs
Overkill for first year.
Ch 5
Series Solutions & Special Functions
Advanced — not needed yet.
Ch 8–24
Vector Calculus, PDEs, Fourier, Complex Analysis…
Later years content. Leave alone for now.
